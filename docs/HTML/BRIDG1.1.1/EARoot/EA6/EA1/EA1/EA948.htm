<p>An IVL<TS> may be comprised of a single range of dates or may be comprised of 2 or more ranges of dates where the first component does not include a value for operator, but where each successive component does.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>This structure allows an IVL<TS> to be defined as the union of, intersection of or difference between two different date intervals.  For example, the first and last week of January could be identified as the first week of January (IVL<TS> where low = Jan 1and high = Jan 7) unioned with the last week of January (IVL<TS> where low Jan 25 and high = Jan 31).  The same timeframe could be represented as the difference between all of January (IVL<TS> where low = Jan 1 and high = Jan 31) and the middle of January (IVL<TS> where low = Jan 8 and high = Jan 24). <br/></p>
