<p>Documents are critical to any protocol-driven research effort.  As a consequence, the THC elected to introduce into the BRIDG Model the core concepts around a 'document representation model' with the recognition that these concepts will be elaborated (and possibly extended) as additional content is brought to the THC for harmonization.  It is critical to recognize that the 'document framework' (shown in PURPLE in this view (to indicate its <<placeholder>> status) represents a structural rather than semantic framework.  The structural model is conceptually drawn from the HL7 Clinical Document Architecture (CDA) specification and can be viewed as consisting of three 'levels' of structure, each with increasing levels of computationally interoperable semantics:<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>DocumentFrame: the 'container' in which all of a given documents contents can be found and attached to which are critical pieces of document information such as type, name, author, etc.  Instances of DocumentFrame can be managed by standard document management methods, be they human-based or machine-based.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>DocumentStructure: the definition of a document's various sections, subsections, paragraphs, etc. is defined by instances of DocumentStructure.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>DocumentContent: the structured (or unstructured/free-text) content of a given document.  In the BRIDG Model, this is the instances of potentially all of the other BRIDG 'red' classes, a fact which will be increasingly explored in future releases of the BRIDG Model<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>As is the case with all classes, the THC introduces the concepts into the BRIDG Model in the hope of stimulating BRIDG community dialogue on the subject.<br/></p>
