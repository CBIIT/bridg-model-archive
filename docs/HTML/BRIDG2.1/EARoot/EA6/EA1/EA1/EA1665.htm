<p>This View shows the core classes that collectively define the domain scoped/defined by the BRIDG model, i.e., <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Protocol-driven research, i.e. the data, organization, resources, and processes involving the assessment of the utility, impact, or other pharmacological, physiological, or psychological effects of a drug, procedure, process, or device on a human, animal, or other biologic subject or substance plus all associated regulatory artifacts required or derived from said research.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Note that the THC has chosen to disambiguate the term "protocol" (and it's various overloaded terms including "trial" and "study" by defining a core class called StudyProtocol whose semantics enable a StudyProtocol to "contain" all of the various documents that collectively define the protocol and all of the activities that define the study.  The View also includes the 'context' classes that are associated with the core StudyProtocol class.  Finally, the class DocumentRelationship enables the documents collected in the course of a protocol's life cycle (i.e. from planning to performing and beyond) to be related to each other with relationship types such as "updates," "replaces," "is a part of," "is an amendment to," etc.<br/></p>
