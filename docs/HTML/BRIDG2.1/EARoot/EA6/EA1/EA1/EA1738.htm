<p>An action plan and execution of a pre-clinical or clinical study in human beings in which individuals are assigned by an investigator based on a protocol to receive specific interventions. Subjects may receive diagnostic, therapeutic or other types of interventions. The assignment of the intervention may or may not be random. The individuals are then followed and biomedical and/or health outcomes are assessed.<br/></p>

public ST
  interventionDescription		 source=ClinicalTrials.gov (Feb 2008) - Intervention Description
Notes: The key details of the intervention. Detailed to distinguish between arms of a study (e.g., comparison of different dosages of drug) and/or among similar interventions (e.g., comparison of multiple implantable cardiac defibrillators). <br /><br />For example, interventions involving drugs may include dosage form, dosage, frequency and duration. (50 mg/m2, IV (in the vein) on day 5 of each 28 day cycle. Number of Cycles: until progression or unacceptable toxicity develops.)

public CD
  blindingSchemaCode		 source=WHO (May 2008) - Masking
source=ClinicalTrials.gov (Feb 2008) - Study Design Masking
Notes: The type of masking used on a study protocol to ensure that the results are not biased by the subjects or investigators.<br /><br />For example, double-blinded would indicate that both the investigator and the participant would not know whether the intervention was a placebo or an active therapeutic intervention.
