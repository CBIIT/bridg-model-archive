<p>A scheduled path through the study which describes, epoch by epoch, what activities the subject will be involved in as they pass through the study.  The group of subjects assigned to an Arm is often called a treatment group.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>For example, a study could have 2 arms named IV-Oral and Oral-IV.  The name IV-Oral reflects a path that passes through IV treatment, then Oral treatment.<br/></p>

Attribute Details
public ST
  name
Notes: The protocol-assigned human-readable name for an Arm in a study design. <br /><br />For example, Treatment A.
public ST
  description
Notes: Human readable text that describes what happens to the subject during the Arm.<br /><br />For example, in a particular treatment regimen, this is a description of the pathway followed by all subjects. <br /><br />For example, "Subjects receive Drug X" or "Subjects receive Placebo."<br /><br />For example, "Subjects receive IV in first arm, Oral in second arm."<br /><br />NOTE: This description should point out what is different between the Arms, if there is more than one Arm.
public ST
  randomizationWeight
Notes: The relative proportion of subjects to be randomized to the Arm.  <br /><br />For example, if 1/3 of subjects are to be randomized to Arm A and 2/3 to Arm B, then the values of randomizationWeightForArm for Arms A and B, respectively, could be expressed as 1 and 2 or as 1/3 and 2/3.
