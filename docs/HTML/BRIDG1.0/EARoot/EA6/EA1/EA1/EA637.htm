<p>The result of observing, monitoring, measuring or otherwise qualitatively or quantitatively recording one or more aspects of physiologic or psychologic processes. <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>For example, in a clinical trial, observations can result from planned activities to address specific questions such as those observations made during a physical examination, laboratory tests, ECG testing, and sets of individual questions listed on questionnaires. <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Not all observations are necessarily planned -- for example, some observations may be laboratory tests that are done in response to patient occurrences that are not planned but used to assess patient symptoms. <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>An observation may be a symptom that a patient experiences that, in the context of a particular clinical trial, will be assessed as adverse. <br/></p>
